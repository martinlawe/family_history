HUCHENNEVILLE 1.5.18
2.5.18		 Parades under Os.C. Companies for training.
The following message from Major General W.G.Henneker C.S. D.S.O. was received
"I desire to thank Captain Pascoe M.C. and the platoon of the 2nd Bn. Rifle Brigade with him, for their splendid work on April 25th in clearing the houses and railway line S.E. of Villers Bretonneux. The operation was carried out with rare dash and gallantry and fully achieved its objective."
The Divisional Commander awarded Parchment Certificates to the following -
2/Lt. W.H.Brann. 2/Lt A.A.Sutton.
3.5.18 The Battalion, less Transport, moved by bus from HUCHENNEVILLE to SALOUEL.
SALOUEL 4.5.18 60 Other ranks joined for duty.
5.5.18 Battalion entrained at SALEUX Station and detrained at FERE EN TARDENOIS and marched to billets at LHUYS
LHUYS 6.5.18 Parades under Os.C. Companies for Training
7.5.18 Parades under Os.C. Companies for Drill and Musketry. Following telegram received from Colonel In Chief in replay to a telegram sent on the occasion of his Birthday. "My best thanks to all ranks, and my heart felt congratulations on their splendid services in the recent fighting of which I and the whole Regiment are proud."
8.5.18 Parades under Os.C. Companies for Progressive Training. A & B Coys. Musketry.
9.5.18 Parades under Os.C. Companies for Progressive Training. A & C Coys. Musketry. The following decorations were awarded to the undernamed Officers for gallantry and devotion to duty West of the SOMME between 22nd March and 2nd April 1918.
DISTINGUISHED SERVICE ORDER Caption G.H.G.Anderson M.C.
BAR TO THE MILITARY CROSS Major A.E. Wass M.C.
THE MILITARY CROSS Captain F.D.R. Milne and 2/Lt, A.J.Sluman.
10.5.18 the BAttalion moved from LHUYS to COURLANDON by march route.
11.5.18 The Battalion moved from COURLANDO to VENTELAY by march route.
12.5.18 Church Parade at 9 am. The Battalion relieved 358th French Infantry Regiment on night 12/13th May. Relief was carried out quietly and no casualties were suffered.
13.5.18 Battalion in front line. All quiet and no casualties suffered. Patrols were carried out at night.
14.5.18 Battalion in front line. Everything very quiet. No casualties.
15.5.18 Battalion in front line. All quiet. No casualties.
16.5.18 Battalion in front line. All quiet. On night of 16/17th May Battalion was relieved by 2nd. East Lancs. Regt. Relief was carried out without casualties, and Battalion moved to billets at GUYENCOURT.
17/5/18 Battalion rested and cleaned up. 81 Other ranks joined for duty.
18.5.18 Parades under Os.C. Companies for Training. Decorations awarded to the following for gallantry and devotion to duty at VILLER BRETTONEUX between 24th and 28th April 1918.
THE MILITARY MEDAL- 40893 Rfn E.Walker 41083 Rfn J.Coit
The Divisional Comander awarded Parchment Certificates to the following for gallantry and devotion to duty West of the SOMME between 22ndMarch and 2ndApril 1918. - Lieut. L.S.Chamberlen M.C. 18160 Rfn J.Ditch 26490 Rfn H. Harris.
Promotion 2nd. B,/ Rifle Brigade. 2/Lt.(A/Capt.) G.H.G.Anderson D.S.O. M.C.(now acting Adjutant) to be Adjutant and to retain his Acting rank vice Lt(A/Capt.) B.C.Pascoe M.C.
19.5.18 Church Parade at 10am.
20.5.18 Parades under Os.C. Companies for Training. 18 Other ranks joined for duty.
On the night of 20/21st May the Battalion relieved the 2nd. Bn. R.Berks.Regt. in right sector of 25th Infantry Brigade front. Relief was completed successfully and teh front was very quiet.
21.5.18 Battalion in front line, all very quiet, no casualties.
22.5.18 Battalion in front line, all very quiet, 1 other rank killed while on patrol.
23.5.18 Battalion in front line, all very quiet, 1 other rank wounded.
3 other ranks joined for duty.
24.5.18 Battalion in front line, very quiet, no casualties.
25.5.18 Battalion in front line, very quiet, no casualties.
26.5.18 Battalion in front line, very quiet, no casualties.
27.5.18 German attack started. Barrage opened at 1am, and Battalion front was heavily gassed and Trench Mortared at 4am. The enemy attacked using Tanks. The Battalion suffered very heavy casualties during the preliminary bombardment and attack. The remnants retired to the 2nd Line and thence over the AISNE by GERNICOURT and held that village till forced to withdraw towards GUYENCOURT. Lieut-Colonel Richardson was wounded. Captain D.Heaton-Ellis killed. 2/Lts. Hoare. Denison. Lowder and Halford wounded.
60 details (Lewis Gun) went up at 12 noon under 2/Lt. Ellis who was killed. 44 other ranks went up under Capt.E.Boughton-Leigh and 2/Lt.J.Nettleton at 8.pm

[It is at this point that Albert Vickers was taken POW]
28.5.18 Remnants of Battalion still in the line.
Casualties estimated at 27 Officers and 680 Other ranks.
29.5.18 Remaining 60 other ranks and 7 Officers under Major A.E.Wass M.C. went to CHAMELAT to form a composite battalion of 25th Infantry Brigade.
Major A.E.Wass M.C and Lt. C.j.Eyston attached to 23rd Brigade to form a Battalion.
The men in Composite Battalion of 25th Infantry Brigade marched to SARCY, they went on and held a line N. of BOULEUSE. Casualties estimated at 3 Officers and 47 other ranks from remainder of Battalion and men in Composite Battalion.
30.5.18 3 Officers and 16 other ranks still with Composite Battalion of 23rd & 25th Infantry Brigades withdrew to SARCY.
31.5.18 3 Officers and 16 other ranks went to CHUMUZY and thence to VANTEUIL with remainder of Composite Battalion.
