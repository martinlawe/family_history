Gilded Age (1877 – 1900)

The Gilded Age was a period of rapid industrial growth, urban expansion, immigration, and economic development. Railroads, factories, mining, and large-scale industry transformed American society, while cities grew rapidly across the country.
Millions of immigrants arrived from Europe during this era, making passenger lists, naturalisation records, city directories, and employment records especially important for family historians. Social differences between wealthy industrialists and working-class families were often extreme.
