The Free State and Early Republic (1923–1949)

Overview: After the Civil War, the Irish Free State stabilized under Cumann na nGaedheal and later Fianna Fáil. Ireland gradually moved away from British influence, eventually becoming a republic in 1949. County Kilkenny: Economic stagnation and emigration continued. Rural electrification and road improvements slowly reached the area. Farming remained the dominant way of life.
