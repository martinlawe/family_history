Restoration and Enlightenment (1660–c.1750)

After years of conflict, the monarchy returned under Charles II, but the balance of power had shifted forever.

This was a period of relative stability, especially in England, with the growth of cities, trade, and early science.

In Ireland, however, the Protestant Ascendancy was firmly established, and Penal Laws restricted the rights of Catholics and dissenting Protestants. For families in both countries, the early 18th century was a time of tightening social control, but also the beginnings of literacy, migration, and commercial opportunity.
