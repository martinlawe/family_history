Reconstruction Period (c.1865 – 1877)

Following the Civil War, the Reconstruction era focused on rebuilding the southern states and reintegrating them into the Union. The abolition of slavery brought major political and social transformation, while industrial growth continued in northern and western states.
For genealogists, this period includes expanding census records, Freedmen’s Bureau records, land records, and increasing state-level documentation. Many families relocated during this time in search of work and new opportunities.
