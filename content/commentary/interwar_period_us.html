USA Interwar Period (1919 – 1941)

The years between the world wars included economic prosperity during the 1920s followed by the Great Depression of the 1930s. Industrialisation, prohibition, migration, and changing social attitudes shaped American society during this era.
The 1920 and 1930 censuses, city directories, employment records, and New Deal documentation provide valuable genealogical information. Many families moved in search of work during the Depression years.
