Civil War and Commonwealth (1642–1660)

Civil war shattered the old order in England, Scotland, and Ireland.

Divided by religion and politics, families chose — or were forced — to side with either Royalists or Parliamentarians. The war’s aftermath saw the execution of Charles I and the rise of Oliver Cromwell, whose campaigns in Ireland were especially brutal, leading to land confiscations and widespread hardship. It was a time of loss, but also one in which older structures of power began to break down.
