Tudor and Stuart Period (1485–1714)

The Tudors - beginning with Henry VII and ending with Elizabeth I, the Tudor period saw major religious change, including the Reformation and the break from Rome. Record-keeping improves significantly, making this a more accessible period for early genealogical research

The Reformation reshaped beliefs and parish structures, and many monasteries — long custodians of local records and land — were dissolved. Families found themselves caught between Catholic and Protestant divides, especially in Ireland, where English plantations and confiscations displaced native landowners. This was also the age of growing state control, overseas exploration, and early education reforms.

The Stuarts - Starting with James I and ending with Queen Anne, this era includes civil war, monarchy restoration, and the beginnings of constitutional monarchy. Parish registers become more consistent, providing increasingly reliable family records.
