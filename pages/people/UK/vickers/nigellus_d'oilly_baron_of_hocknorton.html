Family History

Nigel (Nigellus) D’Oyly

Also known as: Nigel de Oilly, Nigellus d’Oyly, Baron of Hook Norton, Constable of Oxford Castle


Overview


Vital Data

Born: c. 1040–1048, Normandy (approximate)

Died: between 1112 and 1115, probably at Hook Norton, Oxfordshire

Burial: Not firmly recorded

Family

  • Brother: Robert D’Oyly I (d. 1091), companion of William the Conqueror; builder of Oxford Castle.
  • Children:
    • Robert D’Oyly II (d. c. 1142), founder of Osney Priory (1129).
    • Fulk D’Oyly (attested in later genealogies).

Titles & Lands

  • Constable of Oxford Castle (succeeded elder brother Robert I).
  • Lord of Hook Norton (Hooknorton), Oxfordshire — succeeded to the D’Oyly honor after Robert I’s death.

Key Facts

The Oseney Register records that “Robert Doylly the first and Nigel Doylly were brothers … and Nigel begat Robert Doylly the second.”

Through his son Robert II, Nigel’s holdings and local authority continued in Oxfordshire into the 12th century.
