HARIFORT 		Feb 1st to 4th Training as per usual programme continued. Draft of 32 O.R. received on 4th
Feb 4th Battn. and transport marched to MERZEELE starting at 9.ooam and arriving at 11am (approx). Battn went into billets as follows - H.Q. D.1.d.9.6 A Coy D.7.c.5.1. B Coy D.20.c.5.8. C Coy D.2.d.6.1. D Coy D.14.c.8.7. Transport Lines D.15.d.1.10.
Feb 5th to 10th Usual Programme continued. Induction of new class Slignallers continued
Feb 11th Battn. and transport left in conjuction with remainder of Bde and commenced march to relieve 14th Division. Halted for night at ST JAN-TER-BIEZEN. March commenced 11.35am and finished at 4.30pm (approx).
Feb 12th March continued at 9.am, via POPERINGe. New area reached about 1pm. Battn. became No.4 Battn and took over A HUTS in H.2.c.0.8. with transport at Brigade Transport Lines
Feb 13th to 16th Battn. under Coy arrangements practiced Aim (rapid loading and firing) and smoke Helmet drill and new drafts and bad shots carried out some practices on 30yd range in camp. Result of shooting were satisfactory. Bad shots showed increased steadiness and accuracy and ne draft, although they were not altogether at ease, the shooting was by no means bad for recruits. 2nd Lt J.L.CHUMLEY reported on the 15th.

Working party of 400 found by night for new line
Feb 17th Working party of 400 at night
Feb 18th Relieved 11th RB in trenches becoming No." Battn. The 10th K.r.r.'s were on the right flank and 60th Bde on the left.
Disposition of Battn. H.Q. at LANCASHIRE FARM, Front Line - D21, D22, E23. Sopport line The WILLOWS, DAWSON CITY, HEADINGLEY LANE and SPAHI FARM. Reserve line - Canal Bank between bridges 5 and 6. Relief complete at 11.45pm.
Orderly Room was established on the Canal Bank. Enemy was quiet during relief but a M.G. caused some annoyance enfilading front line. Klaxon horns were established at H.Q. DAWSON, CITY, SPAHI FARM and the WILLOWS for the purpose of giving the alarm in event of a gas attack.
It is difficult to describe the trenches taken over as a 'line'. There is nothing continuos in connection about them. Winter and the apparent supemacy of hostile artillery are responsible for this. Both front and Support trenches consist now only of isolated posts - sections of trenches which by good fortune have escaped annihilation. In front in near and on either side of these sections, the ground is literally one series of shell craters. Each now a pool. The earth is often unnatural constency through the frequent explosion of shells and becmes a kind of quicksand after rain.
The parapet where it exists is mostly waist high, never higher, and the ground in front where the theoretical ‘furrow ditch’ should be is a lake varying from a few inches to several feet. There are no communication trenches in the sector and all troops are confined to their positions during daylight. The two great problems — in fact the only problems — in reconstructing the line are first drainage and second uninterrupted artillery supremacy

Trenches feb 19th Enemy infantry quiet/ Artillery activity commenced on both sides with daylight. Enemy fire was chiefly confined to Canal Bank and rear of support line durign morning.
In the afternoon front and support lines were heavily shelled from 11.30 to 2.20pm Covering batteries retaliated and silenced enemy at 2.40pm.
Enemy shelled D.22 with whizzbangs at 3.40pm but stopped on retaliatory fire being opened, no damage was reported. Telephone wires to Coy H.Q. were broken during the shelling and some difficulty was experienced in maintaining communication. Conciderable aircraft activity all day on both sides.
Feb 20th During night 19/20th enemy M.G. fire had decreased but snipers were more active. A few trench mortars were fired by enemy about 9 am, no damage.
Machine Gun which had annoyed and infilated front line was located at C.14.a.8.0. N.C.O.'s patrol reported that enemy had strong parties out apparently working but no noises or sounds could be heard. Enemy patrol approached our wiring party, appeared to be alarmed by something, threw 4 bombs - no casualties and hurriedly withdrew. Patrols reported all quiet on the front. An officers patrol which reconnoitered DILKEM RD and visited a German Bombers or Snipers hole, found it unoccupied - altho' there were signs of recent occupation. Five German Friction Bombs we found and brought in.
Feb 21st Enemy snipers active. Machine Gun referre to previously was silenced temporalily on tese occasions during the night 20/21st by salvos from covering Artillery. During the night enemy patrols did not appear to be paticularly daring and were easily scared. Artillery on bothe sides quieter enemy Machine guns less active.

Trenches Feb 21st
contd		 Relieved by 11 R.B, at night, Enemy quiet during relieft which was safely carried out and completed at 11.15pm. Battn marched to VLAMERTINENE and were billeted for the night in billets lent by 6th Division. Casualties Killed 5 O.R. wounded 28 O.R.
Feb 22rd Moved after dark in to A HUTS, taking up position as No.4 Battn.
A HUTS Feb 23rd to 26th Very little training carried out owing to large working parties found nightly for support snd front line. Owing to an extension of Brigade front a further reconnaissance of the line was carried out during this period
Captn, L.C.F.NASH reported for duty on the 26th for duty
Feb 27th Relieved 11 R.B. in trenches after dark, 1 OR wounded during relief which was completed about 11.45pm. Disposition of Battn. was as follows:- Front Line, B 13. 14 and 15 and FORWARD COTTAGE, Support Line, S 13 and 14, MACGREGORE POST, TOWER POST. Reserve Line X 9 and WILSON'S POST and Canal BAnk. HdQuarters at X.9. Orderly Room Canal Bank. Enemy quiet during night except for a little M.G. fire. Patrols out on B13 reported enemy guard quiet - no signs of work being done
Feb 28th Artillery activity on both sides commenced with daylight, enemy fire was directed mainly on support and reserve lines and Canal Bank.
Feb 29th Usual artillery activity from 10.am, Considerable aircraft activity all day. Patrols reported enemy working in rear of his lines, front clear. Shortly after 1pm. enemy shelled right and rear of B13. with heavy shrapnel and H.E. no damage reported. X9 Fort shelled with Whizzbang and old enemy trenches on right of Fort shelled with Heavy H.E. I13 and 14 eas damaged somewhat by H.E
