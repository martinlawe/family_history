HEBOTERNE 13/1/16 Working parties as on the 12th - morning only
Our guns carried out a bombardment in the morning Chiefly between GOMMECOURT
CEMETRERY and FERME SANS NOM, the 9.2's being very attentive to the trenches between
the CEMETERY and the WOOD. Enemy guns put a few shells on REMAUD and BIRON in the
morning and in answer to our bombardment more on our front line and the BRICKFIELDS.
By night their flares were sent up from their second line.
Weather: a fine morning, a heavy squall in th eafternoon and rain at night.

HEBUTERNE 22/1/16 Working parties as on the 21st, morning and evening
Our guns carried out a bombardment chiefly on GOMMECOURT CEMETERY and 862
The enemy's guns answered vigorously in the final halt-hour, about 150 shells
falling in our section - Field guns, 4.2's and 5.9's. This was followed by quite
a noisy night. Guns, M.G.'s, Rifles, all bombs being fired continuously
Weather:- a fine day. Some rain at night 		Casualties
1 killed

HEBUTERNE 23/1/16 Working parties as on 22nd - morning only.
B and D Companies relieved A nd C Companies by 3.30pm
The Pioneers Section was increased by 4 men per companiy.
The enemy guns were quiet all day except for eight shells which were fired from a new position (south of PUISIEUX)- all "duds"
Weather: a fine day. 		Capt Wingloe njoied the Bn. and was posted to "C" Company
Casualties:-
1 wounded

do 24/1/16 Working parties as on the 23rd - morning and evening
The Guns on both sides were fairly quiet all day. The Battery South of PUISIEUX fired 5 shells in the morning - all "duds" again. Their M.Guns were more active atthe morning "Stand-to".
A patrol in charge of Lieut Cooke (6 men) went out from the BUCQUOY Barricade half way to th eenemy lines: nothing of interest was seen and there were no signs at the "LONE TREE" of a sniper's post
Weater: a fine morning., rain in the afternoon 		Lieut Calder returned from leave, and extension having been granted for medical reasons

HEBUTE 25/1/16 Working parties as on the 24th- morning and evening
A and C Companies relieved B and D companies bt 3.30pm
At 2.0am Three Very Lights were fired in rapid succession from the "16 POPLARS" These were followed by heavy bombardment on the WARWICK trenches and FONCQUEUILLERS:most of the fire came from their batteries near BOIS DE BIEZ. The bombardment lasted about 40 minutes and our batteries too were shelled during the latter part. At 2.20am during the bombardment one of thir patrols threw four bombs into "M" Sap.The Lewis Gun at the Trench End of teh Sap at once opened fire, - no damage was done.
Enemy Guns fired 8 shells in the afternoon near the Shrine - all "duds" again.
Nine hostile aeroplanes passed over our lines in the morning.
A patrol of 6 men from A Company in charge of Lieut's Oldacre and Austin-Leigh reconnoitred the grass and ledge in front of "M" Sap. There were no signs of the enemy occupation there
Weather: a fine day. Cold night 		Casualty:-
1 died of wounds
do 26/1/16 Working parties as on the 25th, plus a new trench near the Bomb Store.
Enemy Guns were quiet all day, but their Machine Guns were more active in the afternoon
Weather: a fine day and very dark at night.
do 27/1/16 The 144th Brigade relieved the 145th in th eusual manner. our last platoon leaving the CEMETERY 1.40pm arriving at AUTHIE 4.30pm - the roads were in better condition for marching. The weather had been fine nearly the whole tour and great improvements had been made in the trenches,
About 7.30pm a Gas alarm was given, reported from "G" Sector, but at 9.0pm all was reported quiet.
Weather: a fine day. 		Lt. Challenor moved to A Coy from D Coy. Lt. Land from A to D Coy
