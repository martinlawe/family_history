G Camp April 1 Coy. Training carried out
April 2 Major W.V.L.PRESCOTT-WESTCARR joined and assumed command of Battn. Coy. training continued.
April 3 Inspection o f Battn. by C.O classes of instruction for N.C.O.'s commenced under B.S.M.
April 4 Training of N.C.O.'s and Coy. programme continued.
April 5 Miniature rage alloted to Battn. bad and indifferent shots and draft were given preference for practice.
April 6 Coy. programme and preferences for practice.
April 7 Usual programme during morning.
At night Battn. moved to CHATEAU TROIS TOURS becoming No.4 Battn.

CHATEAU TROIS TOURS April 8 N.C.O. class continued. Usual working parties of 400 Officers and other ranks found for front line. At night a patrol was sent out from front line (left of B.17) to gain information for a contemplated minor enterprise, in the neighbourhood of MORTELDJE Estaminet.
April 9 N.C.O.'s class continued, usual working parties at night. Patrol again reconnoitred MORTELDJE Estaminet and unfortunately, 2nd Lt. N.S.ROBERTS and 3 O.R. were wounded by a bomb thrown from our own listening post. 2 Coy.'s proceeded to Canal Bank.
April 10 Usual programme continued. Patrol sent out at night as before.
April 11 Battn. moved to relieve 11 R.B. Relief delayed by enemy attack on left sector of Direct Front, but was proceeded with about 9.20pm and completed about 2am without further interuption. Disposition as before.
Enemy fairly quiet, D.20 and D.21 received some trench mortars and catapult bombs during early hours, but all fell short.
Work - only minor repairs were carried out, owing to delay in relief.
April 12 Normal artillery activity, D.21 shelled between 10 and 11am and catapult bombed at night, slight damage done. Patrol encountered enemy patrol in front of B.16. Other patrols reported all quiet.

Trenches Aril 13 WILLOWS shelled with 4.2 during morning, otherwise enemy quiet on Battn. front.
M.G.'s active from High Command Redoubt at night. Owing to bright moonlight our patrols were observed and fired on by enemy. Work on wire, VICARS LANE and drainage.
April 14 Enemy very quiet during morning. D21 trench mortared during afternoon. Enemy M.G.'s active at night - probably in retaliation to our induced M.G. fire. Our Lewis Guns dispersed an enemy wiring party opposite D.21 about 11pm. Patrols reported all quiet.
Work Continued as before.
April 15 Enemy artillery vdy active all day. Between 2 and 4 pm about 150 Trench Mortars fell round TURCO FARM and CLIFFORD's TOWER doing considerable damage. Enemy snipers and M.G.'s very active at night: on our M.G. retaliating enemy openned fire with Schrapnel. Work continued on previous lines
April 16 Enemy fairly quiet during day. Battn, relieved at night by 8th Bedfordshire Regmt. (VI Division). Relief carried out quietly. During this period Battn. was in touch with 10th R.R. on right and right Battn. of 61st Bde on left.
Casualties for period 7th/16th - Killed 5 o.R., wounded 2ndLt. N.S.Roberts and 24 O.R (includes 4 at duty)

April 16 After relief Battn, marched to H.12.b.2.0, entrained, detrained at BRANDHOEK crossing and marched to Camp at E.30.a.5.0 arriving about 3.30am.
CAMP "E" April 17th/18th Battn. marched out at 12 noon, to Camp M, F.27.c.2.6 Where the night was spent, Battn. marched out at 10.20am, via WATOU, HOUTRERQUE and HERZEELE to KICKEN PUT where Battn. went into billets, with H.Q. and transport lines at C.20.d.46. Stragglers Nil.
Billets (KICKEN PUT) April 19th to 27th Following routine was established -Rouse 6am, Physical drill 6.45-7.45am, Breahfast, Parade 9.30 - 12 noon, Dinner, Parade 2-4pm.
Training was carried out under Coy arrangements in Bayonet fighting, open order drill, skirmishing, field fare and Musketry Drill.
A detachment of 2 Officers and 110 O.R were sent to BRIELEN on the 24th for work on the defences.
April 27th Battn. and transport (in conjuction with remainder of Brigade) marched via HERZEELE-HOUTKERQUE-WATOU and JANTER BIEXEN to K Camp (L.4.c.3.5). Time of marching out 4pm, Battn, in camp at 8.15pm, Transport lines L.4.a.3.6. Stragglers Nil.

K Camp April 28 Battn. inspected by C. in C. after inspection the C. in C. congratulated Battn. on its appearance and steadiness and stated that he was very pleased with and greatly appreciated the work done by the Brigade since its arrival in France.
April 29 Regimental classes continued, and usual Coy. training continued. During the afternoon a trench with saps etc. was dug for bombing practice
April 30 Usual programme up to 11.30am. Church Parade 11.45am. Half Holiday from dinner time.
At night the Brigade practised a test march for reinforcing the Canal Bank North of ESSEX FARM. the Brigade marched in column of route starting at Rd. Junctn. L.4.B.9.1 at 6pm and reached the assembly point N.W. of Chateau Trois Tours at about 9.30pm.
Three companies wheeled off to the right thus forming line of Coy.s in fours at 20 paces intervals, At 10pm the Brigade advanced in 2 parallel columns, the left (10 & 11 KRRC) aiming for Bridge 6 and the right (10 & 11 RR) aiming for ESSEX FARM & Bridge 4 serial ditched had to be crossed, but only 2 needed trench boards - These were carried by ponies and greatly facilSitated the advance. The road at B.20.B.7.9 was crossed at 11p,. and he Railway at B.30.A.10.7
