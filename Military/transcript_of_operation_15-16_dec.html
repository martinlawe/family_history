Report on Operations carried out on night 15/16 December 1915
in accordance with 59th Brigade O.O No. 19 and 10th (S) Bn. Rifle Brigade O.C. No. 6.

By 10.30pm on the night 15th December all the parties emmerated in operation Order No.6, 10th (S) Bn. Rifle Brigade, were assembled in the trenches close to the newly constructed Sally Port which had been prepared in our parpet that evening and a lane out through our wire. Signalling communication by wire was established between Raid Headquarters and the Artillery also Raid Headquarters and Battalion Headquarters. Bridging and scaling ladders had been brought up the night before. Axes, electric torches to be fixed to rifles, wire gloves, bomb gloves and wire cutters were dealt out where necessary and countersign arranged. A French horn for recall was given to Lt. Rucker - also a whistle. During the day the machine gun officer had laid two guns on the enemy's parapet on the flanks of our objective and two on the parapet where it was proposed to cut the wire - these later played on the parapet till the wire cutters went out, when they were slightly elevated and kept firing at intervals thereby embrassing the sentries. The original arrangement with the Artillery was for a barrage to be put round the part attacked on receiving the signal "Right curtain" from the O.C. Raid; but due to information recieved during the afternoon from the 59th Brigade an alternative scheme of a more extended barrage was arranged to be applied on the signal "Barrage". The night was dark- moon sets at 1.37am, raining slightly with slight wind blowing from the enemy.
Click here for sketch map At 11.00pm the flank guards, bridge guards with the bridges, wire cutters and signal party went out. Bridge guards placed bridges in position at 'B' and 'D' and the signallers were established at 'C'. The Flank guards proceeded to points 'E' and 'F' at the next ditch afterwards moving forward to points 'H' and 'I'. Lieut Rucker with 6 wire men proceeded to point 'J', which he selected as one point to cut the wire and leaving three men there to cut the wire, he proceeded to 'K' with the other 3. While at 'K' four Germans walking within a few yards of him and went into a listening post just inside the German wire, as in consequence it was impossible to cut the wire at this proposed place, Lieut. Rucker withdrew the 3 wire men a short distance leaving them to watch the listening post and returned himself to report to O.C. Raid. It was decided to make use of one entry only into the enemy's lines.
About 1.55 am bombs and firing was heard on the left. At 2.00am information was sent to Raid Headquarters that the right line had been suffciently cut at 'J' and the wire men had returned to 'F' as arranged unrolling their tape.
O.C. Raid then sent the night raiding and blocking parties to point 'C' to wait further orders as it was evident that the enemy had been disturbed and it was considered advisable to give them time to quiet down.
As the enemy continued to send up flare and it seemed impossible to move any body of men up to the Roman parapet without undue loss it was decided not to attack for the present.
Leaving the two partiesat 'C' Lieut Rucker was sent with a party of bombers to attack the listening post which he successfully accomplished, throwing in 8 bombs from a distance of a few yards, no doubt destroying the inmates, but owing to fire being opened on him from the parapet he was unable to approach to bring back marks of identification.
As enemy appeared thoroughly alarmed it was decided on consultation with Battalion Headquarters to withdraw all parties behind oue own parapet.
This was successfully accomplished bringing all material with them and without any casualties, and the Sally port was closed again before daylight.



Intention. The intention of O.C. Raid was to cut the wire quietly and effect an entrance without previously alarming the enemy.
If this could not be successfully carried out, no attack would be made. In short, a surprise or nothing.

Orders. Distinct orders were given by the O.O.C. 20th Division that such was to be the method of procedure.

Object. The object was not achieved, but the enemy's wire was successfully cut without alarming him.

Outstanding Points
1st. If the wire had been cut earlier the attack could have taken place.
2nd. If there had been no other attack on the same night it could still have taken place.
3rd. The distance between the two lines was perhaps a bit too far, but for many reasons it seemed to be the only available spot.
Timing
4th. With regard to timing the attack - it is quite impossible to be able to accurately judge even within hours how long a job wire cutting under the circumstances will have to tackle, and if too much time is allowed the waiting troops become inefficient as fighters through cold.
Mitigating Circumstances
Placeing the points of attack beforehand.
this was quite impossible -
1st. Owing to our artillery and the enemy continuously changing the nature of the wire on their front, and no patrol could get near their wire for three days beforehand because the enemy had patrols out, and the battalion in the front line was patrolling this front thenselves.
2nd. Spots were picked by O.C. Battalion through glasses but could not be recognised by the wire cutters in the dark.
3rd. The Guards Brigade on our immediate right carried out a small raid only the night before.
4th. Our artillery was continually firing on the German lines for days before the attack and this was not conducive to lulling the enemy into a false sense of security.

Lessons
Two attacks of this description cannot be carried out at the same time close to each other without in the event of being delayed that one suffering through the enemy being aroused by the first one.
