Transcript of 10th Battallion The Rifle Brigade, War Diary July to October 1915

Place Date Time Summary of Events and Information
Canadian Camp. No.12 Larkhill July 21st 3:30pm The Battalion was posted to France, leaving its base at Canadian Camp Larkhill, less transport, in half Battn, entraining at Jamesbury Station for Folkestone
in two train loads, then on to Boulogne. Disembarked and marched to rest camp.
Folkestone Quay July 21st 11:00pm Battn Embark and proceed to Boulogne
Boulogne July 22nd 1:00am Battn Disembark, less transport, and marched to rest camp
Boulogne (RestCamp) July 22nd 12:00 Midnight Battn marched to Railway station and entrained. Transport from Havre was also on this train
St Omer July 23rd Battn and transport detrained and marched to Remilly WIRCQUIM where it went into billets
Remilly July 23rd to 29th Route marches around Remilly
Remilly July 29th Battn marched in Brigade to Renescure. billeted for night in farms
Renescure July 30th Battn marched in Brigade to Bois-La-Notte. billeted for night in farms
Bois-La-Notte July 31st Battn marched in Brigade to Noite-Boom. billeted in neighbourhood in farms
Noite-Boom July 31st to August 1st Company Training
Noite-Boom August 1st All night Battn marched to Lavantie
Lavantie August 2nd to August 9th Battn attached to 1st Scottish Rifles for instructional
pruposes, lectures were given by Officers and A C.O. of Argyll and S'thland H'linds on Trench Warfare, much valuable
information was gained from these.
During this period the men of the Battn receied instruction in the trenches from the 1st Scottish Rifles.
On August 9th the Battn marched to Noite-Boom
Noite-Boom August 9th to August 16th This period was spent in Compant Drill, Rifle Firing practice, respirator and Smoke helmet drill practicing Patrol work and use of physical drill. Bomb and grenade throwing instruction was given to pick men. Part trained Signallers and M.G.S. also received further instruction
Noite-Boom August 16th Batn marched to Laventie and went into billet. Company and squad, Physical and arm drill was practiced, Instruction to M.G.S and signallers continued.
Laventie August 18th Battn moved and took up position as No. 3 Battn. (the diary gives, here, a number of map reference positions)
Work was done daily at these posts, on construction and improvement of paraphets, Firesteps and wire. billets and land around Post where cleaned up.

(Sheet 36.S.W.1)		 August 23rd Battn moved and took up position as No.2 Battn in Trenches. Battn H.Q. Non-Commissioned Officer in charge. Work was done by day on parapets, firesteps dug-outs and Parados, and nightly on burrow-pits in front of parapets
Trenches August 23rd to 30th During this week in trenches the enemy was quiet. casualties were not heavy, nothing of importance happened.
All intellegence acquired was reported to Bgde (59th) daily, detailed progress report was readdressed daily
The weather was good and supplies were received regularly. R.E Material was somewhat scarce patrols were sent out by day and night and observations were forwarded to B'de.
Trenches August 30th The Battn were relieved in trenches and took up position as No.4 Battn in Laventie.
Laventie August 31st to September 6th During this period the battn found working parties for the communications trenches and advance H20 trenches under RE supervision
Laventie September 6th Battn moved and took up position as No.2 Battn in trenches H20 NCO incharge
Trenches September 6th to 13th Work was done daily on parapets, dugouts, parados and revetting same. By night work was done on wire, burrow ditches and saps in front of parapets. No event of importance happened during this period, patrols went out as usual and all information was reported to Brigade as before. Whilst on patrol on Sept 7th with Capt Felton, 2nd lLt Coates was seen by enemy and fired on about 60 yds from enemies line. Lt Coates and Capt Felton were both hit. Felton in 3 places, Lt Coats told Felton to go back as he was done for. Felton crawled back and reported the matter. 2nd Lt Everard went out immediately and found the body, but was forced to return as fire was again opened on him. He reported that Mr. Coates was in his opinion dead. At dusk an Officers patrol went out to recover the body. They reached the spot, but found that the body was missing. From marks in the grass they formed the opinion that the body had been dragged to the German Trenches. Owing to the slope of the ground at this point, which was towards enemy lines it was impossible to see the body or spot from our lines. Casualties 1 dead of wounds 2 wounded 8 all A & F. (Rank and File)
Trenches September 13th Battn moved and took up position as No. 3 battn. Working parties wer provided as before by day and night
M6 d 4.4½
(Sheet 36.S.W.1)		 September 20th Battn. again to trenches as No.2 Battn. HQ also N.C.O incharge. On he 21st and 22nd work carried on as usual. On the 23rd all available were put on making revements, Sally Ports and finishing existing ones. On the 21st in accordance with instructions from Brigade the supporting Artillery opened fire on enemy wire, in front of the Battalion lines, with Shrapnel. The result was reported to be - "Shooting Good", most of the shrapnel burs low and on their wire, posts in some places were blown out of the ground. On the 22nd the enemy were very quiet and appeared to be working very hard on their wire

Trenches (contd) September 23rd Bombardment was continued. During Bombardment enemy apparently evacuated fire trench. Our fire was directed behind enemy parapet. A small fire was caused behind enemy lines. On the 24th enemy were quiet, bombardment was continued at intervals. During the night (23/24) enemy patrol visited our wire, but were disoersed by our fire. Patrols sent out by us cme back and reported that no improvements or anything unusual could be seen. On the 23rd enemy replied to our bombardment with bursts of Shrapnel fire. No damage or casualties. On the 26th The enemy were plainly very uneasy. During the night 25/26 they kept up bursts of rapid rifle fire. No Shrapnel fire. A patrol reported that enemy was working on there wire, but no patrols were seen or heard. On the 26th enemy were very quiet and up to 7pm. (when battn left the trenches). There was nothing further to report. Casualties September 20th to 27th. Killed 3, Wounded 7.
September 27th Battn. moved into billets in Levantie. A & 2 Coys at N.4.b.2.6 and took up position as No. 4 Battalion. During the week working parties were supplied as before and M.G Reserve and Signallers recieved further instruction
September 28th Battn. moved into billets in Levantie and H.Q to Hospice Toulouse M.4.d.6.2
September 30th Capt. H.E.T. Lich of the Battn was evacuated to England Sick
October 1st 2nd Lt G.L. Lich, whilst incharge of a working party was shot in several places by Shrapnel.
October 2nd 2nd Lt L.H.R.Troughton whilst undergoing instruction in Bombing was slightly wounded by fragments
October 3rd Battn. moved and took up position as No. 2 Battn. in trenches A and 2 Coys at M12.c.5.6. On the 4th the enemy were quiet
On the 3rd at 4:30am in response to an SOS call the Battn. stood to, but stood down at 5am. 5/6th everything was quiet. On the 6th and 7th everything was normal On the 8th instructions were recieved and preparations made for an attack. At night Lt H Jones, R.S. with 4 R.E. sappers and escort of 4 went out at 12:30am 9/10/15 to place and fire torpedoes in enemies wire, starting from N12.A.9½.1. They were however fired on at close range by a listening post, the officer and one man being wounded and another man was missing when the party returned. A patrol was sent out to look for him but failed to find him. No other event of importance happened during the week.
On the 9th Capt, H.E. Large, whilst walking along the Rue Tillelay was wounded by a str bullet and died the same day. Other casualties during the period were Killed 4, wounded 13. The Battn. moved on Oct 10th
October 10th Battn. moved and took up position as No. 3 Battn. Coys disposed in ports as follows
A. Dead End. Measselot, Hougement. B. Nangerie, Lonely Post, Road Bend. C. Ports Esquin and Billets. D Billets near White House M12.C.5.6. On the 12th enemy sheelled H.Q. No damage, but interpreter attached (M.G.Flamont) was hit and wounded. On the 13th Coy moved to support trencehes to support 11 RB who were making a front attack. Our artillery shelled enemies fron support trenches heavily and accurately. Enemy replied several shells falling round H.Q. No damage or casualties.

Lavertie October 13th From this date to the 17th when the Battn. moved and took up position as No.2 Battn, the situation was normal. During the week the normal working parties were found for R.E. and repairs carried out in Ports
October 17th Battn. moved and took up position as No.2 Battn. in the trenches. Battn H.Q. at M12,C,5.6. Line held in front line N 14. a. 3.3(left) to M 18. d. 9.1 (right) Forts held, Fremicourt, Félon, and Fauquissart. Enemy front line on this frontage appeared to be strongly held. A few Rifle Grenades and trench motars were fired at our lines. Aim was not always accurate and up to the 21st no damage or casualties were caused by them. About 6.45pm on the 20th two mines were exploded by us oposite N13.5, beyond sending up a large number of flares the enemy did not seem interested. A ditch in N13(approx) was investigated by an officers patrol but did not appear to be used by enemy and was not wired. A 1-pds which fired 5 rounds at our parapet on the 20th was located at N.19.a.4½.9. On the 20th 2nd Lt's Thomas Lancelot, Messil Mearse and Beauford Remmington Hill were accidentally wounded by a bomb exploding, whilst undergoing a course of instruction at Bombing School Laventie. On the night of 21/22 enermy appeared to have large working party out on wire and parapet, and also appeared to be very jovial shouting across to our lines all night. Enemy working parties were continued on the 23rd. A few shells were fired by enemy between M18.d.Y.6 and N13.a.2.2. and enemy were very noisy singing whistling and shouting and talking to our sentries. Casualities during week Killed 1 wounded 2 and 2nd Lt's Hill and Mearse accidently wounded.
October 23rd Battn. moved and took up position as No.4 Battn. in Laventie billets and No.2 as before (H2 Hospice Toulouse M4.d.6.2). On the 24th working parties for R.E. were supplied as usual and one man was killed and 2 wounded whilst working. On the 26th no working parties. Coy drill and physical drill were carried out. On the 26th about 2pm an enemy fired 5 HE shells into Laventie. 3 were blind and 2 burst near Battn. H.Q. Little damage to property but Lt. Stanley Paul Vanstone and Lt and Adjt, George Ralph Frere were hit LT Frere dying the same night. Lt. Vanstone seriously wounded hit in several places and died a few days later. On 26th Lt Roland Hobhouse Thornton was appointed Adjutant

Laventie October 26th Contd vice Lt. G.R.Frere. On the 27th and 28th usual working paries were supplied and arm drill, physical exercise carried out. During the period 23/28 new clothing etc. was received or repaired where necessary. On the 28th the Battn. moved to trenches.
October 28th Battn. moved and took up position as No.2 Battn. in trenches. Frontage and Forts as before and H.Q. Frontage N14.a.3.5 yo M18.d.9.1 (left and right) H.Q. M12.C.5/#.6. Forts held, Fauquissart,Fremicourt, and Félon. During teh night 28/29 enemy were very quiet, work was impossible owing to sodden state of ground, often heavy and continuous rain.On the 29th enemy shelled INDIAN AVE. and RUE TILLELOY at intervals, from 1.30 to 3.15 pm. shelling was very heavy, several trench mortars were fired at Front Line and shells of all calibres from 8 Howitzers down to "Whizz-Bangs" were directed al along Support and Comminucation Trenches. Results, small damage to Parapets, Parados and dug-outs in Front Line and Indian Avenue and Rotten Row damaged in places. A few men were slightly injured by splinters. A number of what appeared to be aerial Torpedos were also fired by enemy at Front Line, these could be distinctly seen coming through the air, were long and pointed in shape and burst with great violence. Work was continued on Parapets and Parados and Dugouts, dugouts and passages drained, footboards laid and damage caused by shell fire repaired.

Laventie October 30th Enemy very quiet. Damage by shell fire in Rotten Row repaired enemy seemed to be jumpy at night and evidently aware of our sapping operations near Red Lamp.
An enemy working party was located opposite N14.1. and Machine Gun fire opened on it by us, result unknown.Dugouts were prepaired for bomb and ammunition Stores and Willow Ditch surviving parallel with Rotten Row cleared.
Parapets in Herring Bones strengthened, bensow ditch at Red Lamp corner continued to trench. Mortars fell behind Parapet at N13.c.2.4 at 10pm no damage. Work on parapets, parados, dug-outs, sally-ports and wire carried on and drainage continued.
October 31st Enemy quiet by day, at night he fired periodical bursts of Rifle Fire. At 6.15pm an enemy patrol was reported to have passed along ceentre Coys Lines at a discreet distance. Saps on right of centre Coys were guarded by standing patrol and listening posts were strengthened and mined saps heads were quarded all night by covering parties. Special work on Red Lamp Corner was carried on continuously and usual work was continued.


What the abbreviations mean

Place Date Time Summary of Events and Information
Front-line Trench The actual fighting trench facing the enemy. Usually zig-zag (not a straight line) to limit blast and enfilade fire.
Parapet The forward wall of the trench (facing no-man’s-land). Built up from earth and sandbags to give protection from enemy fire.
Parados The rear bank of the trench (the side away from the enemy). Gives protection from fragments and blast from the rear.
Banquette / Fire-step / Firing step A stepped ledge inside the parapet soldiers stand on to shoot over the parapet. (Common diary abbreviations: “banq.”, “fire-st.” or “F.S.”)
Firing bay / Loophole A widened section or a shaped opening in the parapet for rifles or a machine-gun to fire through.
Revetment Timber, corrugated iron or sandbag facing used to hold the trench sides and steps in place. You’ll see “rev.” or “revet.” in notes.
Traverse An earth/sandbag barrier across the trench to prevent shrapnel and blast traveling along the trench; makes the trench zig-zag. (May be abbreviated “trav.”)
Duckboards Wooden boards laid on the bottom so men didn’t stand in mud/water. Often mentioned with “sump” or “drain”.
Typical dimensions and layout (typical, approximate) Trench depth (standing depth): ~6–8 ft (1.8–2.4 m) from ground level to trench bottom; varies.on ground and threat.
Banquette height above trench floor: ~2 ft (0.6 m) (so a man can step up to fire).
Traverses: placed every 20–50 yards depending on ground and threat.
Barbed-wire belts: tens of yards out in front in belts and irregular patterns. (These are representative figures — diaries seldom record exact measurements.)
