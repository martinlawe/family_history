Trenches Mar 1st Artillery on both sides quieter. Aircraft active from about 11am to dark enemy artillery confined itself to targets on and near Canal Bank, bulk of fire was whizzbangs
Mar 2nd To assist operations of Vth Corps at ST ELOI, Battn. stood to at 4am and opened rapid rifle and M.G. fire in conjuction with bombardment of enemy lines by covering artillery. Enemny immediately responded with Artillery of all calibres and heavy M.G and rifle fire. This was kept up until 4.45am when enemy fire ceased and situation became normal shortly afterwards. During the day enemy was very quiet but watchful. Battalion relieved by 12 R.B(60th Brigade) at night. Relief carried out without interuption and Battn. entrained at H.12.b.2.0. detrained at BRANDOER Crossing (G.12.b.6.8.) Marched to B HUTS G.5.d. (Ref Sheet 28 1/40,000) Casualties for period Feb 28th to Mar 2nd 11 OR wounded
B HUTS Mar 3rd to 10th During this period of rest all ranks recieved instruction in method of fixing and laying trench wire as an obstacle and Smoke Helmet drill and rapid fire were carried out. As far as possible clothing and necessaries damaged and lost in the trenches were replaced. Baths were alloted to and made the fullest use of by Battalion.

B HUTS Mar 10th Entrained at BRANDHOEK Crossing (H.12.b.6.8) and detrained at H.12.b.6.20 and proceeded to Trenches to relieve 6th Kings Shropshires L.t (60th Brigade). Relief carried out without incident and completed at 1.30am 11/3/16. Disposition as follows- Front Line b 16 -2½ platoons. B17-1 platoon, S 18 a - 1 platoon, Willow WALK 1 Platoon, D20 1 platoon, D21 -1 platoon, Total 7½ platoons. Support Line S.16.b - ½platoon. S.15.b - 1 Platoon LA BELLE ALLIANCE- 3 platoons. WILLOWS (left and right) 4 platoons total 8½ platoons. Battn. H.Q. LA BELLE ALLIANCE. Note References and names of posts given are approximate and local. It is not possible to give exact refernences because of isolated positions of trenches Line is not connected in any way and consists of odd sections of trenches that have escaped destruction, and are not marked on trench map.
Mar 11th Enemy remarkably quiet during early hours and seemed to be working hard. Accomodation in front line was insufficient and the few remaining hours were devoted to constructing improvised and temporary shelters for men. Some difficulty was experienced in locating Bomb posts and dug-outs in front and support lines, these might be conspicuous by day but had to be found by night. Artillery became active at day light. A bombardment of enemy's salients at C.13.b.0.8 (approx) by our artillery bought heavy enemy retaliation on D.20,D.21 and the WILLOWS causing some damage to dugouts parapets and wire. Work commenced by outgoing unitmin construction of VICARS LANE (new support line) was continued. Enemy showed more activity during early part of night with rifle and M.G, fire but after 10pm this practically ceased. Patrols were sent out on whole of front, nothing of importance was reported.

TRENCHES Mar 12th A light gun duel took place during the morning, enemy shelling D.20 and D.21. In the afternoon these points received a few heavy shells. Attitude of enemy at night was vicious from 6 to 10pm. he kept up M.G. and rifle fire along whole front. about 10.15pm an enemy patrol about 20 strong approached our listening post 150 yards front of B.17. 2nd Lt. S.W.FORTUNE and two bombers went out from B.17 to investigate and appears to have encountered enemy sooner than they anticipated, 2 bombs were thrown from inside and a bomb simultaneously thrown by enemy wounded the 2 men, 2nd Lt. FORTUNE appears to have been shot point blank and his body carried away. A strong patrol went out immediately but were unable to overtake enemy.
Work was hindered by enemy M.G. fire somewhat, but never ceased. work was continued. new instructions received from Brigade on, wire, reclaiming, revetting and strengthening parapets.
Mar 13th Front and Support lines intermittantly shelled with schrapnel and H.E. during early hours, durign the day enemy quiet, only a few Whizzbangs fell in Battn. sector. After dusk enemy opened up a bombardment of all ground in rear of trenches with field and Machine Guns and rifle fire, all open expanses of roads, all works and positions and dumps came in for special attention. Later Machine Guns opposite D.20 considerably impeded work at that point. Artilery retailiation had no apparent effect, although suspected points were fixed as patrols reported nothing of importance.
Work continued on previous lines in front line in new support line wiring continued.

Trenches Mar 14th Enemy quiet on Battn,. front and his artillery confined itself to targets in rear of line. Aircraft active during morning. Usual Machine Gun and rifle fire up to 10pm. Battn. relieved by 11th Rifle Brigade, relief carried out quietly and completed at 1.10am. During period 10/14th Mar Battn. were in touch with 61st Bde. on left flank and 10th Kings Royal Rifles on right flank. Salvage parties were sent out nightly to collect abandoned stores, equipment etc. All material collected was returned to Brigade H.Q. A system of signalling to supporting Batteries by means of firing up red very lights from different places in Battn, sector was tried. Lights were fired from 5 points but it seems improbable that they could be seen by observers at Battery position chiefly because they do not rise high enough; also lights fired from No.'s 3 and 4 positions - as viewed from Battn. HQ., seemed to rise from the same place.
All rations had to be carried from a dump near Canal Bank to the Line in sandbags, reserve platoons from LA BELLE ALLIANCE were used for this work. It was found impossible to keep fires burning in front line during daylight as any signs of smoke drew hostile artillery fire. Hot meals were therefore, out of the question, and preserved

March 1916 meat only was supplied. Some oil stoves were purchased regimentally and solidified alcohol - burners and issued from Ordinance. Oil was drawn in the usual manner, and by this means it was found possible to provide the men with hot tea and soup daily. All drinking water was brought up, in petrol tins, with rations. Some difficultly was experienced in maintaining a supply of water owing to a shortage of tins.
Casualties for period. Killed 2 O.R. wounded 10 O.R. Missing 2nd Lt. S.W.FORTUNE. Draft of 12 O.R joined 13/3/16 but were kept at transport lines until Battn, was relieved in the Trenches.
Mar 14 After relief Battn. marched to BRIELEN CROSS ROADS, embarked and were conveyed to H.8.a.3.1, disembarked and marched to A HUTS H.1.d and H.2.b
A HUTS Mar 15/17th Owing to heavy night working parties (400 to 450 per night) very little training was possible during this period

A HUTS Mar 17 Moved to CHATEAU TROIS TOURS B.28.a.6.0. Usual working party of 400 at night
CHATEU TROIS TOURS Mar 18 Moved to relieve 11 R.B. in Trenches. Whilst Battalion was marching out of Chateau an incident occure which suggested espionage. The enemy burst about 40 rounds of heavy schrapnel between Chateau and road and of avenue to main road. It may have been a coincidence but the Chateau had never before been shelled at night and schrpnel was obviously intended to catch moving troops as occupants would be inside at night. Fortunately no casualties were caused. Enemy followed Battalion up to Canal Bank with hids fire even shelling tracks across the fields. Bridge 4 and ESSEX FARM were heavily shelled whilst relief was in progress. Relief completed about 1.45 am. Enemy activity ceased about 1 am. After relief work consisted filling and stacking sandbags for use on following nights.
Trenches Mar 19 Enemy artillery strangely quite, aircraft very active by day. Usual M.G. fire up to 10.30pm. Hostile patrols reported active but not daring. Patrols reported enemy working hard but bright moonlight prevented closer investigation. Work - strengthening and improving parapets and wire, drainage and laying footboards in front line, drainage and revelting of support lines.

Trenches Mar 20 B.16. shelled spasmodically otherwise Battn. sector quiet during day, Up to 10pm enemey's attidude normal then on he became exceptionally quiet. Although every effort was mde it was found impossible to draw enemy's fire - possibly because a relief was taking place. Our patrols met or saw no enemy but heard sounds of working. Work B.16 Comminuication fairly weel established along trench and revetting secured, Stengthening, revetting and clearing of support line continued.
Mar 21 Enemy passive up to 1pm when B.16 was shelled with Whizzbangs and trench motars, some damage to parapet, dugouts and trench, artillery retaliation was asked for and freely given: enemy silenced about 1.45pm. Enemy Machine Guns more active at night, but ceased about 11.30pm Ly. C.E.S. Rucker wounded. Work- damage to B.16 repaired, strengthening and revetting of parapets and drainage, improving and strengthening wire leaning of support line continued.
Mar 22 Enemy fairly quiet but shelled WILLOWS intermittently. Usual M.G. fire up to 10pm. Relieved by 11th R.B. relief completed about 1.20am. After relief Battn. marched to Chateau TROIS TOURS. becomimg No.4 Battn. Period 18th - 22nd Battn. had 10thK.R.R. on right fank and Battn. of 62st Bde on Left flank. 1 officer wounder=d and 13 O.R. wounded

Chateau de TROIS TOURS Mar 23/26 Night working parties of 400 to 450 Officers and men continued , consequently very little training was done. On the 24th Lt. Col. St. J.D.Loftus was invalided to England. 2ndLt. S.J.PEGLER joined Battn. on the 26th
Mar 26 Moved to relieve 11 R.B. in line. There was no repetition of incident reported on 18th
Relief completed at 12.45 am. Disposition as before, very little work done, thorough inspection of work in progress and of wire in front carried out. Patrols reported enemy invisible and judging by sounds he was working in rear of his lines.
Trenches Mar 27 Enemy quiet up to 4 pm; at that hour he opened a vigourous bombardment on Battn.'s left sector, the WILLOWS and VICARS LANE recieving particular attention. Possibly enemy's object was to destroy the new work in this vicinity; if so, the attempt failed for very little damage was done in spite of heavy shells used and only one casualty, one man wounded, Enemy less active during early part of night. Patrols sent out along ADMIRAL'S Rd. were fired on and bombed by enemy, and a Sergeant was wounded. The Officer i/ch of patrol (2nd Lt. S.J.PEGLER), after ordering the remainder of patrol to open rapid fire to cover his retirement, carried the wounded man in, and returned, and bought remainder back safely. Work was impeded by intense darkness and bad weather. Wiring continued and damage in VICARS LANE and WILLOWS repaired.

Trenches Mar 28 Enemy passive on Battn. front, his artillery was active in neighbourhood of Canal Bank. At night enemy appeared jumpyduring early part of night but quietened down by 11pm. Our patrols met some of enemy and reported all quiet but saw signs of new work in enemy lines. Work- wiring, revetting and strengthening of parapet continued, drainage system enlarged to support line. Lieut. R.W. Hatch joined.
Mar 29 Enemy whizzbanged line intermittently at different points from 10am to 4pm. Left of VICARS LANE again shelled mostly with 4.2 damage small. Attitude of enemy normalat night. Usual patrols sent out, nothing unusual reported. Work - cleaning,, revettingand drainage, usual wiring parties out, and damage to VICARS LANE repaired.
Mar 30 Considerable aircraft activity, D.21. whizzbanged about 1.45pm. About 2.30pm about 6 Germans were seen to leave their parapet opposite. Lewis Gun and rifle fire was immediately opened upon them and they retired. Meanwhile this party had been spotted by artillery O.O. and Battery fired about 20 rounds of schrapnel. About dusk a strong enemy patrol was observed in front of B.16. A party was sent out with a Lewis Gun and opened fire, enemy immediatelyretired. Battn. was relieved by 6th Ox and Bucks (60th) Brigade, relief completed by 11.30pm.
