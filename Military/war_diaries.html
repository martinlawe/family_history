Below are the transcripts of the War Diaries for the 10th Battalion The Rifle Brigade running from July 1915 through to May 1916, when Albert Edward Vickers was wounded in Action on the 29th May 1916. It is understood that Albert did not return to the 10th Battalion, as he was hospitalised through to December 1917, when a comment is found in the Windsor and Eton Express. When he returned to the Front in 1918 it was with the 2nd Battalion. The transcripts of their War Diaries are also below.

Note:-Where, within these Diaries the term Battn. moved to No.X. this is identifying where the battalion was in the order for the front line, No.1 being in the trenches at the front.

It should also be noted that I have not included the Casualty Lists, that were created at the time, as I see no benefit in doing so.
