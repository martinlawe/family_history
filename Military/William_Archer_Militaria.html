Family History

William Archer

1898 - 1917

Military Service

Service Number:-R/6280
Regiment:- 12th Battalion King's Royal Rifle Corp



William Albert Archer served as a Rifleman in the 12th (Service) Battalion, King’s Royal Rifle Corps during the First World War. His service number, R/6280, indicates that he was a wartime volunteer, part of the large New Army formations raised in response to the outbreak of war.

The 12th Battalion landed in France in 1915 and served continuously on the Western Front. By 1917 it formed part of 60th Brigade in the 20th (Light) Division, a formation composed largely of light infantry and rifle regiments and frequently committed to demanding front-line operations.

In the summer of 1917 the battalion was engaged in the Third Battle of Ypres, one of the most prolonged and costly British offensives of the war. Conditions in the Ypres Salient were exceptionally severe, with heavy rain, deep mud, and constant artillery fire making even routine movement dangerous.

On 16 August 1917, William’s battalion took part in the fighting associated with the Battle of Langemarck, a major phase of the Ypres offensive. British units attacked heavily defended German positions across waterlogged ground, sustaining significant casualties from shellfire, machine-gun fire, and counter-attacks.

William Albert Archer was killed on that day. His death occurred during a period when the battlefield was densely occupied by British divisions, with battalions frequently holding adjacent sectors or rotating through the same trench systems. Other rifle regiments, including battalions of The Rifle Brigade, were operating in the same general area at this time.

William’s death reflects the experience of many New Army soldiers in 1917, fighting in appalling conditions for limited gains at immense human cost. He was one of countless men whose lives were lost in the mud and shell-fire of the Ypres Salient.
