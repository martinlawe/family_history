SAILLY Dec 1st Coy drill and medical inspection and inter-platoon football. Brigade Boxing competition was proceeded with in the afternoon
Dec 2nd battn. was inspected by G.O.C. 20th division near CUL DE SAC FARM G.8.d.7.6. After inspection G.O.C. stated that he was very pleased with the men's appearance and with the hard work that the Battn. had done. After calling attention to a few minor details re dress the G.O.C. dismissed the parade.
Dec 3rd Battn. alloted baths at ESTAIRES. Working party of 135 all ranks found for R.E..
Usual programme was continued by Coys.
Dec 4th Battn. moved and took up position as No.1 Battn. in front line. Took over from N.9.2 exclusive to 3rd run-up .E. of KNIGHTSBRIDGE AVE in fire trench with A and B Coys. C and D Coys remained in support. C Coy at H.Q. and D Coy in billets N.2.l.8.3. H.Q. N.2.d.4.6. Trenches were taken over from 7th KOYLI (61st Bde). Trenches were very flooded and communications very shaky and falling in in places, PINNEY AVE and CELLARFARM AVE being inpassable,"V.C"AVE was waterlogged in places.Battn. was in touch with 11th KRR on the left and 2nd GUARDS Bde on the right. Enemy were quiet but their aircraft showed some activity between 12 Noon and 3pm. At night a thorough examination was made of our wire and patrols sent out to examine enemy'e wire, NO MANS LAND and enemy frontage for litening posts etc. Patrols reported NO MANS LAND to be impassable and found a large number of ditches all waterlogged and varied in size and depth.

Trenches Dec 4th Cont.d Enemy wire was found to be about 30 yds from his parapet ad to consist of concertina and chevaux de prise and was in good condition. Our patrols met no enemy or saw any working party, but enemy could be heard driving in stakes about N.9.d.8.2.
Owing to the frequency of enemy shelling all rations and R.E. materials were sent up to Front Line after dark by Coys in support.
Dec 5th Enemy quiet, his aircraft showed activity between 12 and 3pm but were kept back by our A.A. guns, aircraft flew high over our lines. Patrols sent out confirmed reports as previously given by those of the 4th. Our artillery bombarded enemy front line opposite N.9.c.4.8. damaging it and causing a small fire.The shooting was accurate but some of the Howitzer shells were blind. The explosions sent masses of sandbags, earth and timbers into the air. Enemy retaliated with schrapnel on RUE PETILLON a patrol examined RUE DELVAS between ours and enemy line, they found a disused sandbag baracade and (apparently) sentry post and at N.9.d.1.4. rows of concertina across access road.
Dec 6th Enemy Infantry quiet, his artillery was active against V,C,CORNER (N.2.d.0.2) with HE and schrapnel, a direct hit was obtained by him on barn in EXE POST setting fire to straw inside and afterwards the barn caught fire. All bombs, rations and S.A.A. in post were saved. Enemy appeared to be busy baling out trenches. Our aircraft was active over enemy lines about midday
Dec 7th Enemy snipers and Machine Guns active. Patrol closely examined enemy wire from N.9.d.9½ to N.9.c.4½.1½ and reported it was very strong on places and to consist of curtains and aprons

Trenches Dec 7th Cont.d Our artillery shelled emeny wire and parapet opposite N.9.4 effectively from about 12.30 to 1.15pm. enemy parapet was badly knocked about.
Dec 8th Enemy very quiet and still busy baling out his trenches. Patrol reported enemy working on his wire and NO MANS LAND to be very muddy and swampy. Our artillery shelled enemy lines between N.9.c.8.2 and N.9.d.5.2 from 12 noon to 2pm. Enemy retailiated by sending 3 shells into our parapet and one in a dugout in N.9.2 with small damage, he also directed heavy rifle and M.G. fire over N.92 to N.9.4. Enemy front was patrolled between N.9.d.8.4. and N.9.d.8.3½ without encountering any enemy working parties or patrols and reported NO MANS LAND to be very swampy. Casualties for period Killed 1 Wounded 6.
Dec 9th Battn. moved and took up position as No.3 Battn. with H.Q, at N.26.d.6.7. A and C Coys at WINDY POST (N.1.a.10.7) and billets D Coy in CROIX BLANCHE POSTH.33.a.5.1 and B Coy in billets at N.33.c.4.4.B and D Coys supported left battn. (10th K.R.R) in front line and A and C Coys supported right battalion (11th R.B.)
Dec 10th Working parties of 175 for R.E. and 200 for the front line. Remainder of Battn. on Coy drill and cleaning up.

FLEUR-BAIX Dec 11th Usual parties for R.E. and front line and remainder of battn. on Coy drill
Dec 12th R.E. and front line parties found. Remainder on rapid fire and arm drill.
Draft of 50 O.R. arrived. mostly composed of wounded men returned, some being serving men from 3rd and 4th R.B. physique being superior to that of previous reinforcements.
Dec 13th Battn. moved and took up position as No.1 battn. front line with A and B Coys, C and D Coys being in support. Disposition as on Dec 4th. 11th K.R.R. on right and Guards Brigade on the left. Enemy were quiet and patrols reported no sign of enemy patrols or working parties. Enemy snipers very quiet. Work in progress was continued and wire strengthened. Trenches and communications in flooded condition.
Dec 14th Enemy infantry quiet, enemy shelled V.C.CORNER at intervals. No damage, Work done on drainage and footboards laid in fairway. A listening post was selected with a view to a minor operation and sally port cut in parapets. Map references 36 S.W.1.(French map)
Dec 1th Enemy sniped at weak positions of our parapet but were afterwards subdued by our snipers. Our artillery bombarded positions of enemy lines and he retaliated rather weakly on our support lines damaging V.C. AVE. in places. At night a minor operation was projected in the form of a raid on enemy lines, in accordance with 59th Bde O.O.19

Trenches Dec 15th The object was to be a surprise and raiding parties were to return before enemy had time to appreciate the situation and develop his counter attack. Battn. O.O. for raid and accounts of operation are attached.

Dec 16th Attitude of enemy was normal at 4am but he continued to send up large numbers of flares until daylight. During the day he was quiet, after dark he kept up intermitent Rifle and M.G. fire and sent up large numbers of flares. Enemy fire was particularly heavy between 6 and 7.30pm and forced our wiring parties to retire.
Dec 17th Enemy very quiet by day but continued to keep up fire at night and to send up large numbers of flares. Casualties for period Killed 2 Wounded 3.
Dec 18th Battn.moved and took up position as No.4 Battn. with H.Q. at G.36.d.9.8.
Coys as follows — A (billets) G.36.d.5.8. Estaminet ROUGE DE BOUT. Coys as follows A (billets) G.36.d.5.8. Coy H.Q. at G.36.b.7½.½. B JUNCTION POST and billets at H.32.a C. WINTERS NIGHT POST and billets H.31.b and d, D Coy billets and CHARRED POST N.6.b.9.2.
Dec 19th Working party of 54 found for carrying Gas cylinders to front line. Remainder of Battn. on Coy drill with rapid fire and smoke helmet drill and cleaning up.
Dec 20th Working parties for front line and Gas carrying found. Coy drill and arm drill continued

Dec 21st to 24th Large parties found for R.E. and front line, repairs to forts and usual programme of training by Coys continued

Dec 25th Working party of 45 found for R.E. after Church Parade no training done.
Dec 26th Working party of 80 found for R.E. Short Route march by Coy's after Church Parade
Dec 27tth Working party of 125 found for R.E. Short Route march and squad drill. Signallers did flag drill.
Dec 28th Drill under Coy arrangements, instructionof Signaller continued. All N.C.O's were drilled by B.S.M. Working parties of 125 found.
Dec 29th Usual programme by Coy and practice of flag drill by Signallers continued. A reconnaissance of BOIS GRENEIR defences was carried out by Brigadier aid
