AUTHIE 1/2/16 Working parties , 25 from each Company, were found for Hurdle Making
The rest of the Battalion practiced attack in the morning
Weather:- a fine and cold day 		Capt. M. SIMMONDE 36th Horse, Indian Coy. joined the Battalion for a week & was posted to "C" Company
do 2/2/16 The 145th Brigade relieved the 144th Brigade in the usual order and at the same lines - B and D Companies to Front Line A Company to the Keep, C Company to the Chateau.
Working parties were found in the evening on all the communication Trenches
A Conference of Company Officers was held at Brigade Headquarters at 4.30pm.
The enemy Guns were quiet, Except for some shelling in the afternoon but their Machine Guns were much more active.
Weather :- a fine day

HEBUTERNE 3/2/16 Working parties, morning and evening, on all communication Trenches
The enemy guns were again quiet except for some shelling in he afternoon
Weather:- A cold day with rain at night
do 4/2/18 Working parties, morning only.
"A" and "C" Companies relieved "B" and "D" by 4.0pm. Our guns carried out an arranged bombardment on th eenemy's trenches opposite our right section, at 2.0pm. They replied at once with about 150 shells and at 6pm they put about a dozen into the village.
A special day-light reconnaisance of the enemy's wire was made by Lieut's CALDER and TEED from 10.15am to 1.30pm. The reconnaisance was carried out very successfully and teh information the acquired was of great value.
Weather: Rain fell most of the day and night.

HEBUTERNE 7-2-16 Working parties as on the 6th - morning and evening.
Enemy machine guns showed usual activity. Their guns fired about 50 4.2's on our two left trenches at 11.30am; these shells came from a Battery which up to the present had fired on the next Division on our left.
Weather: A fine day with rain at night
do 8-2-16 Working parties as usual, morning only.
"A" and "C" Companies relieved "B" and "D" Companies by 3.30pm, at 7.50am Their guns began firing and did not cease until dark, the shells falling chiefly in the village, Brickfields and No Road.
All their guns were firing, from field guns to 8.2's, and there was a most marked absence of "duds". Their shooting was accurate. A very trying day - about 3,000 shells were fired on the Brigade front during the day.
A patrol in charge of Lieut TEED went out at 9.0pm from "M" sap returning about 11.0pm. They reconnoitred the front of our RIGHT Section but all seemed quite quiet.

HEBUTERNE 9-2-16 Working parties during morning and evening.
Enemy's guns were again most active on our front all day, about 2000 shells of different calibres falling during the day - another worrying day.
Weather: Snow fell in the early morning: fine day with hard frost at night. 		Lt. Lund retd
from leave.
Casualties:-
2 wounded
do 10-2-16 B and D Companies relieved A and C Companies by 3.30pm.
All the enemy guns were again active, shells falling chiefly in the village, Battalion Reserve Line and the Communications trenches
Our heavy guns fired on PUISIEUX and BUCQUOY and the enemy at once shelled SAILLY and HEBUTERNE. One of their shells burst on one of the Brickfield's Dugouts; There were seven men inside and although no one was actually killed they were all very much shaken up - a most fortunate escape
Weather: a fine day 		Casualties

do 11-2-16 No working parties were possible today as enemy guns kept up their incessant fire. Most of the shells fell in the village and outer defences. For the last two days they have been firing much heavier guns. Another worrying day, but their guns were not quite so active.
Weather: A wet day with snow and sleet. 		Lts Payne &

HEBUTERNE 14-2-16 B and D companies relieved A and C Companies by 6.0pm
Our guns carried out an arranged bombardment lasting most of the day. The Enemy replied chiefly with their 5.9's.
A conference of Commanding Officers was held at Brigade H.Qtrs at 4.30pm to discuss the ne dispositions
Weather: A wet day and night
At 11.0pm the enemy opened rapid fire on the division on our Left, lasting about 10 minutes and from midnight till dawn our guns and theirs were firing continuously. 		Capts Boyle

do 15-2-16 The new dispositions came into force at noon,the 4th OXFORDS, the left Battalion remaining in their present position and of the three other Battalions one was out for four days, the remaining two taking the additional front between them. We hold our old front plus four fire trenches on our Right.Our sector was divided into two, each company with three platoons in the firing line one in immediate support and each having the call if two platoons from one of the reserve companies. The company in the KEEP was brought up as Battalion Reserve.
Enemy guns were quiet all the morning but their field guns and 5.9's were busy in the afternoon.
Weather: A rough showery day and very wet night.
Colonel W. P. Seccoldt, V.D. vacated the command of the Battalion on February 14th, having received an order to report himself to the WAR OFFICE as soon as possible. His leaving was a real lose to the Battalion for he had put in 32 years Service in the Regiment. 9 years as Commanding Officer.
The news of his leaving was received with the regrets of all ranks Major R.J. Clarke took Command of the Battalion

A and C Companies relieved B and D Companies by 6pm.
At 3.30am our guns put in 150 shells into the CHEMIN CROUX. Enemy Machine Gun and Rifle fire was very heavy in reply chiefly from their front line judging from the amount of fire from their front line at morning and evening 'Stand-to', their line is much more strongly held than previous.

do 19-2-16 B and C Companies relieved A and C Companies by 6.0pm
At 1.30am The enemy opened a very heavy bombardment sent on the Brigade on the left lasting for about 1¼ hours. At 6.30pm they opened another heavy bombardment sent on the Brigade on the Right, lasting about 45 minutes; Gas shells were being fired.
Their working parties in front of us were very busy and many words of English were heard as "hurry up" "put it down there" "dig that hole deeper" "bring those plate along", etc.
About 11pm two of their aeroplanes passed over the village going North
Weather: a fine day and night
