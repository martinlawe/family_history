Trenches Nov 1st Enemy quiet, continuous rain, trenches and support lines from 2 to 3 ft deep in mud and water. Enem fired about 10 H.E. shells at Indian Avenue. This trench was in a very bad condition through collapses and damage by shell-fire. Work was continued on Red Lamp Corner under instruction from Brig-Gen and damage caused by weather and shell-fire repaired as far as possible.
Nov 2nd Enemy unusually quiet, no improvement in weather, dugouts parados and parapets collapsing, work was continued as before, as far as possible.
Nov 3rd Enemy quiet, weather still bad and works still collapsing. Trenches practically unpassable and work almost impossible. Relieved by 11 RB 3 to 5 pm
RUE BACQUEROT Nov 3rd Battn, took up position as No.3 Battn. H.Q. M.18.a.o.y. Coys on Forts and billets as follows. A Coy (right supporting Coy, supporting 11 RB) billets near White House (M.12.c.5.6). B Coy (left supporting Coy, supporting 10th West Riding) Fort Esquin and billets near N.11.c.4½.5.(approx). C Coy. Posts Wangerie ( M.17.C.8½.3) Road Bend (M. 17.d.6`¾`.1`¾`) Lonley Road (M.17.a.6.9). D Coy posts Dead End (M.6.6.o.3) Hougement (M.12.c.8.7½) Masselot (M.12.a.4.6½).
Nov 4th to 6th Weather fine, working parties were found for support and front line under R.E. supervision. A draft of 1 officer (Lt. C.E.S.RUCKer) and 14 men arrived on the 4th.
Nov 7th Good weather, no working parties, enemy shelled RUE BACQUEROT, between Wangerie and Lonely Lane, no damage.
Nov 8th to 10th Fine. Working parties as before, enemy shelled support lines daily. Battn. relieved by 11 RB

RUE BACQUEROT Nov 10th Battn, took up position in trenches as No.2 Battn. H.Q. M.12.c.5.6. Front LIne held N.14.a.3.5.(left) to M.18.d.9.1.(right) in touch with 61st Bde(left) and 11 Kings Royal Rifles(right). During the night enemy were quiet and work was begun on new trench maintenance scheme and constructing new type dugout under R.E supervision.
Nov 11th Enemy quiet but then artillery shelled RUE TILLELEY and land in rear of support lines, apparently searching for our Batteries. 2 enemy planes crossed our lines flying very high about 9am. before enemy commenced shelling., they were engaged by our A.A. guns. A patrol examined ditch at N.13.c.2.4 and ditch parallel to our parapet at this point. Both were full of water and impossible for use by enemy patrols. No sign of enemy patrols or working parties were seen. Work was continued under R,E, supervision.
Nov 12th Enemy very quiet, he sent about a dozen Rifle grenades into M.18.d, out trench Motar Batteries replied. Two pigeons were observed fliying over Rue TILLELAY and apparently settled just behind German Lines about 4.40pm. Enemy could be heard hammering in stakes between N.19.a.4.6 and N.13.d.0.3 and seemed to be working on his wire. An enemy covering party was observed going in by our listening post at 10pm. Patrol in front of N14.1 saw no enemy but heard him working on his parapet up to 11.45pm. Enemy was heard hammering in stakes opposite Red Lamp Salient at 3.15am. M. G.fire was opened on this point and work ceased. Worlk continued on new dugouts under R.E. supervision

Trenches Nov 13th Enemy very quiet, they were heard driving in stakes at N.19 a.4.9 at 10.30pm.sniping decreased a hostile M.G. traversed our parapet,oposite Drury Lane at 9.15pm. Our M.G replied after which enemy were silent. A patrol examined enemy wire from N.13.c.8.2 to N.13.c.6.1. and found it intact, about 3' 6" high and evidently new. Work on new scheme under R.E. supervision was continued.
Nov 14th Battn. moved and took up new position in trenches. A and B Coys only in front line, C Coy going into Hougemont Post and D Coy into Dead End Post. H.Q. did not move, a patrol went out over new area at night, but saw nothing of importance. An enemy covering party was observed on front of crater in N13.5. Patrol threw a bomb, causing some confusion among enemy, some of whom ran away. Working parties were sent out and made a thorough examination of the our wire along the whole front.
Nov 15th Enemy quiet, about 4.15am he was heard hammering in stakes oposite Picanton Communication Trench and at 10am was pumping water from th efront line at N.14.a.2.4.2. Enemy planes flying high crossed our lines about 9.20am and though engaged by our A/A guns they were manouvering upa nd down (apparently) in the vicinity of our front and support lines for some time, one being particularly persistent. Enemy fired 125 H.E shells (4.2 and 5.9) at heavy Battery across road from Battn. H.Q. between 10.15 am and 12 noon, of which 22 were blind. as far as could be seen thee was no material damage. Work was continued on new scheme and wire was improved and strengthened.

Trenches Nov 16th Enemy quiet all day, but directed 2 bursts of M.G.fire at G.N.R. asilhead on RUE TILLELAY about 4.30pm. Only intermittent Rifle fire at night. Enemy very busy pumping water from his front line opposite N.14.1 all day
Nov 17th Battn. were relieved in trenches by 11 R.B. and moved to Laventie into billets as No.4 Battn. H.Q M.3.d.6.0 (with 83rd Coy R.E.) Coys were billeted as follows. A and D Coys as Battn. H.Q. B Coy In huts M3.d.4.1 and C Coy 3/pltns M.4.d.6.2 1 pltn with Battn. H.Q.
LAVENTIE Nov 18th Divnl. Baths were alloted to Battn and all Coys were sent. The remainder of the day was devoted to cleaning, drying and brushing clothing and equipment and repairing and fitting clothing. A working party of 300 N.C.Os and men with Officers was furnished for R.E. at night to dig a new communication trench.
Nov 19th A working party of 60 men and N.C.O.s by day. Remainder Battn, did Coy and Platoon drill, physical and smoke helmet drill and rapid fire practice. All Coys went a short route march in the afternoon and the M.G.P recieved further instruction

LAVENTIE Nov 20th Intructions were received from Bde that Battn. must be prepared to move at short notice after 10.30am 21/11/15. All programmes of work were cancelled accordingly and Coys were drilled as far as possible within billets, all ranks wore Battle Order from 10.30am, first line transport stood by, and ammunition ponies and horses for Field Kitchens were sent to Coys. A Coy had Musketry practice on 30 yd range, and in the afternoon, after asking Brigade, Bombing classes were continued. Situation was announced normal about 4 pm. Meanwhile a minor operation was carried on opposite 61st Bde front. The Artillery bombarded enemy front line from N.10.c.2.2 to N.10.c.9.6 with objective of destrying Mine shafts and doing as much damage as possible to personnel and material. Machine Guns co-operated with Artillery, no infantry action was contemplated but the 59 Bde were held in rediness to move if needed.
Nov 21st Church Parades were held for and attended by all denominations and in the afternoon all Coys went for a short route march. B Coy practiced Musketry on the 30yd range.
Nov 22nd and 23rd Usual programme of Coy training carried out.
Nov 24th In accordance with 59 bde.Q.Q.15, Battn. with transport moved to new Billets at SAILLY in conjuction with remainder of Bde. Move was carried out by Coys at about 30 minute intervals. Bde went into Divisional reserve on arrival and Battn became No.3 Battn or support Battn.

SAILLY Nov 24th New disposition of Battn. as follows. H.Q. G.16.d.3½.0, Transport lines G.21.a.4.6½, A Coy G.23/b/1/2., B Coy G.22.b.3.10, C Coy| G.16.c.8.4, D Coy Tents G.22.c.4.9
A programme was issued by Brigade for the period of reserve by which Battns. were placed at the disposal of Q.C's until 28/11/15 by which it was suggested that training should be arranged to leave afternoons free for the men to carry out a series of sports which had been arranged.
Nov 25th All Coys had half an hours running drill before breakfast, followed by a short route march from 9.30 - 12 noon. The afternoon was devoted to Football.
The Divisional Theatre of Sailly was opened at night. A working party of 100 men was found for R.E
Nov 26th Running Drill and route-march before dinner and Football in the afternoon. Working party of 200 found for R.E.
Nov 27th Route-march and inter-platoon football morning and Brigade paper-chase afternoon. Battn. finished second.
Nov 28th Running drill and route march in the morning, Afternoon football final tie for Brigade Cup. Battn. Team were beaten by 11 KRR Battn. finished 2nd in competion
Nov 29th Coy drill and bombing practice. Inter-platoon football afternoon.
Nov 30th March in column by half battns. Distance Judging was practiced en-route and visual training was given. Football in afternoon.
