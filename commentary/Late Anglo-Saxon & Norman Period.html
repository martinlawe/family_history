Late Anglo-Saxon & Norman Period (c.1040–1154)

This early period was marked by the end of Anglo-Saxon England and the dramatic upheaval of the Norman Conquest in 1066. For families living in England, this meant a shift in rulers, landownership, and even language. The Normans introduced new systems of governance and castle building, and land was redistributed to loyal followers of William the Conqueror.

In Ireland, life continued under native Gaelic rule, relatively untouched by these events — for the time being.
