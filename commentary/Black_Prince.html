Black Prince

Edward of Woodstock, The Black Prince (1330–1376)

Who was he?

  • The eldest son of King Edward III and Queen Philippa of Hainault.

  • Born on June 15, 1330.

  • Though he never became king (he died before his father), he was the heir apparent and Prince of Wales.

Why "The Black Prince"?

  • The nickname "Black Prince" appeared after his death; the origin is uncertain.

  • Possibly from the black armor or heraldry he used, or from later chroniclers romanticizing his image.

Military Achievements

  • A brilliant military leader during the Hundred Years' War against France.

  • Famous victories include:

    • Battle of Crécy (1346) — where the English army won decisively using longbowmen.

    • Battle of Poitiers (1356) — where he captured the French King John II.

  • His tactics and leadership cemented his reputation as a warrior prince.

Titles and Holdings

  • Made Prince of Aquitaine in 1362, ruling over a large part of southwestern France.

  • Known for chivalry and patronage of the arts.

  • He was also a key figure in the order of the Garter, England’s premier chivalric order.

Personal Life

  • Married Joan of Kent, a notable noblewoman known as the “Fair Maid of Kent.”

  • They had two sons, including Richard II, who later became King of England.

Death and Legacy

  • Died at the age of 45 on June 8, 1376, likely from illness.

  • Buried in Canterbury Cathedral.

  • Left a legacy as a symbol of knighthood, chivalry, and military prowess.
