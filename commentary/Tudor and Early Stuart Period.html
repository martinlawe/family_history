Tudor and Early Stuart Period (1485–1649)

The Tudors brought strong monarchy, religious turmoil, and major changes to land and church life.

The Reformation reshaped beliefs and parish structures, and many monasteries — long custodians of local records and land — were dissolved. Families found themselves caught between Catholic and Protestant divides, especially in Ireland, where English plantations and confiscations displaced native landowners. This was also the age of growing state control, overseas exploration, and early education reforms.
