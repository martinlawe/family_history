Historical Context

Notes.

In the 1851 Census, Isaac is living in Love Lane, and he was recorded as a Chaff Cutter, a role involving the preparation of animal fodder by chopping straw and hay — vital work on farms or estates to feed working horses and cattle. By 1881, he was listed as a General Labourer, reflecting a broader range of physical work as the town of Windsor expanded and rural occupations shifted.
